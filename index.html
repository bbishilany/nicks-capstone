What Is the Ohio Division of EMS?

  • Part of the Ohio Department of Public Safety
  • Administrative arm of the State Board of Emergency Medical, Fire, and Transportation Services (EMFTS)
  • Located at 1970 W. Broad Street, Columbus, OH 43223
  • Key responsibilities:
  • Certification of EMS and fire service providers and instructors
  • Accreditation and chartering of EMS and fire training institutions
  • Approval of EMS continuing education sites
  • Regulation of medical transportation services
  • Lead agency for Ohio's trauma system

Website Overview — ems.ohio.gov

  • The central hub for all EMS and fire certification information in Ohio
  • Main navigation sections:
  • Certifications — initial, renewal, reinstatement, reciprocity, verification
  • Education & Testing — training facilities, CE requirements, testing options
  • Programs & Services — trauma system, grants, community resources
  • About Us — State Board info, newsroom, policies, strategic plan
  • Help Center — agency directory, forms, contact info
  • Hosts downloadable course packets, OAC rule references, and scope of practice documents

EMS Certification Levels in Ohio

  • Emergency Medical Responder (EMR) — OAC 4765-12 — Entry-level; basic emergency care and CPR
  • Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) — OAC 4765-15 — BLS care, patient assessment, medication administration
  • Advanced EMT (AEMT) — OAC 4765-16 — IV access, advanced airway management, select medications
  • Paramedic — OAC 4765-17 — Full ALS care, cardiac monitoring, drug administration, intubation (min. 500 hrs didactic/lab + 400 hrs clinical/field)
Firefighter Certifications
  • Volunteer Firefighter — State minimum for volunteer service
  • Firefighter I — 160-hour course; NFPA 1001
  • Firefighter I & II — 244-hour combined course
  • Firefighter II Transition — 84 hours (existing FF I)
  • All through Ohio chartered fire training programs
Specialty Certifications
  • Fire Safety Inspector — Code enforcement and inspection
  • Fire & EMS Instructor I — Classroom instruction
  • Fire & EMS Instructor II — Course design & program mgmt
  • Live Fire Instructor — Required for live fire training
  • Hazard Recognition Officer

Fire Instructor Certification — OAC 4765-21

  • Prerequisites — must hold valid firefighter certification
  • Complete a fire instructor training course (minimum 60 hours)
  • Includes 4-hour fire service training module
  • Includes 4-hour live fire training awareness module
  • From the "Ohio Fire and EMS Instructor Curriculum"
  • Pass the fire instructor knowledge examination
  • Pass the instructional methods examination
  • Complete 10 hours of supervised teaching (min. 6 hrs classroom + up to 4 hrs practical)
  • Course provided under accredited/chartered training program, taught by Instructor II

Key OAC Chapters — Title 4765

  • OAC 4765-12 — Emergency Medical Responder certification
  • OAC 4765-15 — EMT certification
  • OAC 4765-16 — Advanced EMT certification
  • OAC 4765-17 — Paramedic certification
  • OAC 4765-18 — Fire and emergency services courses
  • OAC 4765-20 — Certification renewal and continuing education
  • OAC 4765-21 — Instructor certification
  • OAC 4765-24 — Fire service courses (additional requirements)
  • OAC 4765-25 — Definitions (incorporated standards by reference)
EMS CE Requirements
  • EMR: 16 hours of CE per cycle
  • EMT: 40 hours of CE per cycle
  • AEMT: 50 hours of CE per cycle
  • Paramedic: 60 hours of CE per cycle
  • Testing in Lieu of CE option (NREMT exam)
  • Ohio refresher course option
  • Maintain current NREMT registration
Fire Instructor Renewal
  • <12 months: No CE or instruction required
  • 12-24 months: 2 hrs CE + 8 hrs instruction
  • 24-36 months: 4 hrs CE + 16 hrs instruction
  • Submit renewal application before expiration
  • Comply with firefighter cert requirements
  • Hours approved by fire chief or program director

Website Tools & Resources

  • Certification Verification — look up any Ohio EMS/fire provider's cert status
  • Ohio Fire Online Training Service (OFOTS) — 200+ online courses including 70+ fire protection
  • Training Facility Directory — find accredited/chartered programs statewide
  • Agency Directory — contact info for regional EMS agencies
  • Downloadable Course Packets — FF I, FF II, Transition, Inspector
  • Scope of Practice — downloadable PDFs for each EMS level
  • Newsroom — regulatory updates (e.g., new Ohio Fire Code, Nov. 2025)

Programs & Services

  • Trauma System — lead agency for Ohio; trauma/burn center directory
  • Ohio Trauma Triage Pro — mobile app for field triage decisions
  • Naloxone Information — resources for opioid overdose response
  • Grants — funding opportunities for EMS agencies and training
  • Community Resources — public education and outreach materials
  • EMS/Trauma Data Requests — data for research and planning
  • Complaint Form — public reporting for provider issues
  • EMFTS Strategic Plan — long-term goals for Ohio EMS and fire services
